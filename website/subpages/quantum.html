The Quantum Model

Quantum mechanics differs from the standard model in 3 main ways.

1. Quantities are restricted to discrete values (Quantization)

2. Objects have both wave and particle properties (wave-particle duality)

3. There are limits to the accuracy with which one can predict physical properties prior to its measurement given its initial state. (Uncertainty principle)

Instead of showing exactly where something is, such as an electron in a hydrogen atom, a quantum mechanical representation of the hydrogen atom gives probabilities of where the electron could be, without stating where exactly the electron is.
